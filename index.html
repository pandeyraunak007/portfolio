How I Think About Product

Talk to users obsessively

Every meaningful product decision I've made started with a conversation, not a dashboard. The AI Data Modeler exists because I talked to data modelers daily and heard the same frustration repeatedly. Insights hide in conversations, not spreadsheets.

Measure what matters, then question it

I built analytics dashboards that drove 30%+ engagement growth ‚Äî but I also know metrics can mislead. The best PMs know when to follow the data and when to trust their judgment about what the data isn't capturing.

Simplify relentlessly

Enterprise software loves complexity. I don't. A 25% reduction in support tickets came from simplifying UX flows, not adding features. The hardest PM skill is saying no to good ideas in service of great ones.

AI is a tool, not magic

I've shipped LLM-powered features that reduced delivery time by 30%. But I also defined guardrails, success metrics, and human-in-the-loop safeguards. AI works when you're honest about what it can't do.
